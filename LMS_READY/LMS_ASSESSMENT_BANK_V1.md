## 📚 SECTION A: OHS ACT SECTION 8 & EXECUTIVE LIABILITY (Questions 1 - 10)

### Question 1: General Duty of Care in Remote Environments
*   **Question**: Under the 2026 'Reasonably Practicable' legal standard (South African OHS Act Section 8), does an employer's general duty of care extend to distributed remote home-offices?
    *   [ ] A) No, it only applies to physical corporate headquarters.
    *   [ ] B) Only if the employee is using corporate-provided hardware.
    *   [x] C) Yes, it extends fully to remote home offices, requiring active physical and psychosocial hazard management.
    *   [ ] D) Only during standard office hours (08:00 - 17:00).
*   **Feedback**: Correct. The 2026 Department of Employment and Labour standard formalizes that hybrid home-offices constitute classified remote work environments under Section 8.

### Question 2: Administrative Penalties (Section 38)
*   **Question**: Failure to enforce active home-office ergonomic safety audits exposes executives to what maximum administrative penalty under the OHS Act Section 38(1) Amendment?
    *   [ ] A) Up to R50,000 fine.
    *   [ ] B) Up to R100,000 or 6 months imprisonment.
    *   [x] C) Up to 10% of annual group turnover or R5,000,000 (whichever is greater), alongside personal criminal liability.
    *   [ ] D) A standard compliance warning with zero financial exposure.
*   **Feedback**: Correct. The Section 38 amendment introduces strict corporate liability, penalizing executive negligence with fines up to R5M or 10% of annual turnover.

### Question 3: Section 37 (Vicarious Liability)
*   **Question**: Under Section 37 of the South African OHS Act, how can an executive board legally protect itself against vicarious liability for safety breaches committed by representatives or contractors?
    *   [ ] A) By verbally instructing representatives to work safely.
    *   [ ] B) By signing an informal waiver at the start of employment.
    *   [x] C) By executing formal written Section 37(2) agreements and keeping continuous, audited compliance records.
    *   [ ] D) Vicarious liability cannot be managed or mitigated under any circumstances.
*   **Feedback**: Correct. Written Section 37(2) agreements formalize the transfer of health and safety duties to avoid automatic corporate vicarious liability.

### Question 4: Reasonable Practicability Factors
*   **Question**: What are the core factors used to determine whether a safety measure is "reasonably practicable" under Section 1 of the OHS Act?
    *   [ ] A) Only the financial budget and company profitability.
    *   [ ] B) The personal preference of the site manager.
    *   [x] C) The severity of the hazard, the state of knowledge available, the suitability of mitigations, and cost versus risk.
    *   [ ] D) The geographical distance of the remote worker.
*   **Feedback**: Correct. "Reasonably practicable" weighs severity, knowledge, means of mitigation, and cost-benefit ratios.

### Question 5: Health & Safety Representatives (Section 17/18)
*   **Question**: In South Africa, an employer must designate at least one Health and Safety Representative (SHE Rep) for every how many employees in a general office environment?
    *   [ ] A) Every 20 employees.
    *   [ ] B) Every 50 employees.
    *   [x] C) Every 100 employees (or fraction thereof).
    *   [ ] D) Only when requested by a labor union.
*   **Feedback**: Correct. Section 17 mandates at least one rep per 100 employees in office shops and one per 50 in other workplaces.

### Question 6: Employee General Duties (Section 14)
*   **Question**: Under Section 14 of the OHS Act, what is a primary legal obligation placed on the employee?
    *   [ ] A) To conduct independent monthly financial audits.
    *   [x] B) To take reasonable care of their own safety, use provided PPE, and report unsafe conditions immediately.
    *   [ ] C) To design safety policies for the entire organization.
    *   [ ] D) To refuse all training that occurs outside core work hours.
*   **Feedback**: Correct. Section 14 mandates employee cooperation, safe personal conduct, and reporting of hazards.

### Question 7: CCMA constructive dismissal and digital tethering
*   **Question**: If a company consistently contacts remote employees late at night, resulting in psychosocial burnout, the CCMA may classify a resignation under what legal heading?
    *   [ ] A) Voluntary redundancy.
    *   [ ] B) Unilateral contract variation.
    *   [x] C) Constructive dismissal due to failure to provide a safe working environment.
    *   [ ] D) Uncontested mutual separation.
*   **Feedback**: Correct. Intolerable stress from digital tethering violates Section 8, opening paths for constructive dismissal claims.

### Question 8: ISO 45003 Focus Area
*   **Question**: What is the primary objective of the ISO 45003:2021 global standard within an occupational health and safety system?
    *   [ ] A) To standardize machine shielding for mechanical presses.
    *   [ ] B) To define the chemical composition of fire extinguisher foam.
    *   [x] C) To provide guidelines for managing psychosocial risks and promoting mental health at work.
    *   [ ] D) To govern corporate vehicle fuel emission caps.
*   **Feedback**: Correct. ISO 45003 is the leading standard for psychosocial safety, mental well-being, and workload fatigue management.

### Question 9: Section 24 Incident Reporting Timeframes
*   **Question**: Within what timeframe must an incident causing serious injury, loss of limb, or death be reported to the Department of Employment and Labour under Section 24?
    *   [ ] A) Within 7 working days.

### Question 10: Mandatory Safety Training SAQA Alignment
*   **Question**: OHS safety representatives must be trained under courses aligned with which NQF Level framework to be considered fully accredited?
    *   [ ] A) NQF Level 1 (General Certificate)

## 🛢️ SECTION B: FORECOURT HAZARDS & SPILL RESPONSE (Questions 11 - 15)

### Question 11: Frontline Fuel Spill Evacuation Protocol
*   **Scenario**: A class-1 petroleum spill of over 50 liters is identified on the service station forecourt near a customer vehicle. What is the immediate first-action sequence for the forecourt attendant?
    *   [ ] A) Retrieve paper OHS manuals from the back office to review spill containment steps.
    *   [x] B) Trigger the emergency shutdown valve (ESD), evacuate the immediate zone, and deploy dry-chemical powder (DCP) and spill sorbents.
    *   [ ] C) Wash the spill down the general stormwater drain immediately with water.
    *   [ ] D) Ask the customer to move their vehicle to clear the lane.
*   **Feedback**: Correct. Frontline emergency spill response requires immediate ESD lockdown, dry-chemical powder deployment, and zone evacuation to prevent static ignition.

### Question 12: Static Electricity Ignition During Petroleum Refuelling
*   **Question**: Why are static grounding wires and strict "no-cellphone" policies enforced on petroleum service forecourts?
    *   [ ] A) Cellphones interfere with digital pump calibration tools.
    *   [x] B) To eliminate potential static charge differences that could ignite volatile hydrocarbon vapors.
    *   [ ] C) To prevent employees from being distracted during transactions.
    *   [ ] D) Cells drain pump backup generators.
*   **Feedback**: Correct. Grounding and avoiding spark sources minimizes the hazard of static electricity igniting petroleum gases.

### Question 13: Hazardous Chemical Substances (HCS) Safety Data Sheet (SDS)
*   **Question**: Where must the Safety Data Sheet (SDS) for hazardous fuel additives and solvent cleaners be kept?
    *   [ ] A) Locked in the regional manager's offsite safe.
    *   [x] B) Prominently and freely accessible on the shop floor or forecourt where the chemicals are handled.
    *   [ ] C) Stored in the vehicle glovebox of the delivery driver.
    *   [ ] D) Shipped in a zip file only accessible to IT.
*   **Feedback**: Correct. Under the HCS regulations, SDS sheets must be immediately available to any employee handling the substance.

### Question 14: SANS Symbolic Safety Signage
*   **Question**: According to SANS 10140 guidelines, what shape and color code designates a mandatory safety action (such as wearing safety boots)?
    *   [ ] A) Red triangle with white symbol.
    *   [ ] B) Green square with white cross.
    *   [x] C) Blue circle with white symbol.
    *   [ ] D) Yellow triangle with black border.
*   **Feedback**: Correct. SANS mandatory signs are blue circles with white icons. Red is for prohibition/fire, yellow for warning, and green for safe conditions.

### Question 15: First Aid Response Sequence (DRSABCD) CPR Ratio
*   **Question**: During first aid resuscitation (DRSABCD), what is the correct compression-to-breath ratio when administering CPR to an unresponsive adult?
    *   [ ] A) 15 compressions to 5 breaths.
    *   [ ] B) 5 compressions to 1 breath.
    *   [x] C) 30 compressions to 2 breaths.
    *   [ ] D) Continuous compressions without any rescue breaths.
*   **Feedback**: Correct. Standard adult CPR protocols mandate 30 compressions to 2 rescue breaths.

## 📊 SECTION C: RISK IDENTIFICATION & PROBABILITY MATRICES (Questions 16 - 20)

### Question 16: The 5-Step Risk Assessment Calculation
*   **Question**: How is a task's absolute Risk Rating Score determined using the standard OHS 5-Step Risk Matrix?
    *   [ ] A) Risk = Severity - Probability
    *   [ ] B) Risk = Severity + Frequency
    *   [x] C) Risk = Probability (1-5) x Severity (1-5)
    *   [ ] D) Risk = Total Incidents / Total Hours Worked
*   **Feedback**: Correct. Under NQF Level 2 SHE representative guidelines, the Risk Score is the product of Probability (Likelihood) and Severity (Consequence), mapping ratings from 1 (Very Low) to 25 (Extreme).

### Question 17: CCMA "Digital Tethering" Active Mitigation Gate
*   **Question**: Under ISO 45003 psychosocial guidelines, what active mitigation gate proves an employer took all 'Reasonably Practicable' steps to prevent after-hours cognitive burnout?
    *   [ ] A) Sending a weekly wellness email advising staff to disconnect.
    *   [x] B) Implementing active digital gateway locks (Fatigue-Gate and Cognitive Handshakes) that restrict off-hours network access and log compliance telemetry.
    *   [ ] C) Providing free gym memberships to remote workers.
    *   [ ] D) Banning the use of email during the day.
*   **Feedback**: Correct. Continuous digital compliance gates (Nelly's Fatigue-Gate and Cognitive Handshake) log admissible evidence of employer risk management.

### Question 18: Hazard vs. Risk
*   **Question**: What is the fundamental difference between a "Hazard" and a "Risk"?
    *   [ ] A) A hazard is hypothetical, whereas a risk is an active incident.
    *   [x] B) A hazard is something with the potential to cause harm; a risk is the likelihood and severity of that harm occurring.
    *   [ ] C) A hazard is a chemical issue, while risk refers only to slip-and-trip occurrences.
    *   [ ] D) They are identical terms under the South African OHS Act.
*   **Feedback**: Correct. Fuel is a hazard; smoking near the fuel creates a high-risk scenario.

### Question 19: Risk Severity Matrix Ratings
*   **Question**: In the standard 1-5 severity matrix, how is a severity score of 5 defined?
    *   [ ] A) Minor scratch requiring a plaster.
    *   [ ] B) Lost time injury of 1-3 days.
    *   [ ] C) Reversible injury requiring standard clinical treatment.
    *   [x] D) Catastrophic event causing single or multiple fatalities, or massive environmental destruction.
*   **Feedback**: Correct. Rating 5 represents catastrophic severity (fatalities or major toxic releases).

### Question 20: The Hierarchy of Controls
*   **Question**: When mitigating a forecourt chemical exposure risk, what is the correct hierarchy of controls from most effective to least effective?
    *   [ ] A) PPE -> Administrative Controls -> Substitution -> Engineering -> Elimination
    *   [x] B) Elimination -> Substitution -> Engineering -> Administrative Controls -> PPE
    *   [ ] C) Administrative Controls -> PPE -> Elimination -> Engineering -> Substitution
    *   [ ] D) SANS Signage -> PPE -> Evacuation -> Fire Drills -> Incident Reports
*   **Feedback**: Correct. Elimination is the most effective engineering solution, and personal protective equipment (PPE) is always the final line of defense.
